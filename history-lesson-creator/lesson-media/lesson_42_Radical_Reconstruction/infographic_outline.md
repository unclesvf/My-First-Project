### 2. Constitutional Conventions
Early 1868. South Carolina holds a constitutional convention to write a new state constitution. For the first time, Black men participate in government.

### 3. The Fourteenth and Fifteenth Amendments
The constitutional changes aren't just at the state level. Congress is rewriting the U.S. Constitution to protect Black rights.

But white conservatives won't accept this new order. They use violence to restore white supremacy and undo Reconstruction. The Ku Klux Klan—a terrorist organization of former Confederates—spreads across the South.

## Key Vocabulary

- **Radical Reconstruction:** Congressional plan (1867-1877) imposing harsh terms on South and protecting Black rights.
- **Fourteenth Amendment:** 1868 amendment granting citizenship and equal protection to freed slaves.
- **Fifteenth Amendment:** 1870 amendment protecting voting rights regardless of race.
- **Reconstruction Acts:** Laws placing South under military control until new constitutions guaranteeing Black voting rights.
- **Ku Klux Klan:** Terrorist organization using violence to restore white supremacy and intimidate Black voters.
- **Hiram Revels:** First Black U.S. Senator, representing Mississippi during Reconstruction.
