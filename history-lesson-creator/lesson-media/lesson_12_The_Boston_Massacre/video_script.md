## Chapter 3: The Trial and Propaganda
*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
The soldiers were arrested and tried for murder. In a surprise move, John Adams—a patriot who opposes British policies—agreed to defend them. He believes even British soldiers deserve a fair trial and a good lawyer. At trial, Adams argued the soldiers faced a violent, threatening mob and fired in self-defense.

## Chapter 4: The Aftermath
*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
The day after the shooting, acting Governor Thomas Hutchinson met with angry town leaders. Thousands of armed colonists gathered on the Common. The message was clear: remove the troops or face an uprising. Hutchinson, fearing a full-scale rebellion, ordered the British regiments to withdraw to Castle Island in the harbor.

## Chapter 5: A Turning Point
*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
In the months that followed, an uneasy quiet settled over Boston. Most of the Townshend Acts were repealed—except for the tax on tea, which Britain kept as a symbol of their right to tax us. The troops were gone from our streets. Life returned to something like normal.
