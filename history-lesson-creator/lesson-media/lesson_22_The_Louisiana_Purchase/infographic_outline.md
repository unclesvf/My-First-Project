# Infographic Outline: The Louisiana Purchase

**Subtitle:** Doubling the Nation (1803)

**Time Period:** 1803

## Sections

### 1. A Territory Changes Hands
I'm Marie, fourteen, living in New Orleans. My city has been French, then Spanish, now French again, and today I learned we're about to become American! The news arrived: President Jefferson has purchased all of Louisiana from Napoleon for $15 million.

### 2. New Opportunities
The Purchase solves major problems for America. We needed access to New Orleans—the mouth of the Mississippi River where western farmers ship their goods. France controlling it was dangerous.

My city, New Orleans, is unlike anywhere else in America. We're French and Spanish, Catholic and Creole. We speak French more than English.

### 5. The Price of Expansion
While Americans celebrate acquiring vast new territory, few ask about the people already living there. Tens of thousands of Native peoples call Louisiana home: Osage, Caddo, Choctaw, Chickasaw, and dozens of other nations. France just sold their land without their consent.

## Key Vocabulary

- **Louisiana Purchase:** The 1803 purchase of Louisiana Territory from France for $15 million, doubling U.S. size.
- **Napoleon Bonaparte:** French leader who sold Louisiana to the United States to fund wars and prevent British seizure.
- **New Orleans:** Strategic port city at the mouth of the Mississippi River, crucial for western American trade.
- **Mississippi River:** Major river system that western farmers used to ship goods, now entirely under U.S. control.
- **Strict Construction:** The belief that the Constitution should be interpreted literally, limiting federal power to what's explicitly stated.
- **Empire of Liberty:** Jefferson's vision of America expanding westward as a nation of independent farmers.
