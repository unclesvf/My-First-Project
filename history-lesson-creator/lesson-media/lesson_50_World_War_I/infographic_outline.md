# Infographic Outline: World War I

**Subtitle:** The Great War (1914-1918)

**Time Period:** 1914-1918

## Sections

### 1. The Great War Begins
I'm James, eighteen years old, and I'm an American soldier in France. The war has been raging since 1914—four years of brutal combat that has killed millions and devastated Europe. The war started with the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in June 1914.

### 2. Over There—American Doughboys in France
"Over there, over there, Send the word, send the word over there, That the Yanks are coming, the Yanks are coming... And we won't come back till it's over, over there."

George M. Cohan's song captures American enthusiasm as we entered the war.

### 3. The Tide Turns
By spring 1918, Germany launched a massive offensive, trying to win before American troops arrived in force. They broke through Allied lines, advancing toward Paris. For weeks, the outcome was uncertain.

### 5. Coming Home to a Changed Nation
The Senate rejected the Treaty of Versailles and refused to join the League of Nations. Wilson toured the country promoting the treaty, but suffered a stroke that left him partially paralyzed. Without his leadership, the treaty died.

## Key Vocabulary

- **World War I:** Global conflict (1914-1918) between Allied and Central Powers.
- **Trench Warfare:** Brutal WWI combat from fortified trenches, causing massive casualties.
- **Zimmermann Telegram:** German message to Mexico proposing alliance against U.S., helping push America into war.
- **Woodrow Wilson:** President who led America in WWI and proposed League of Nations.
- **Fourteen Points:** Wilson's peace plan including self-determination and League of Nations.
- **League of Nations:** International organization Wilson proposed to prevent future wars—U.S. Senate rejected it.
