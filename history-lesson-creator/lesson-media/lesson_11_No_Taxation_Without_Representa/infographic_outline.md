# Infographic Outline: No Taxation Without Representation

**Subtitle:** The Stamp Act Crisis (1765)

**Time Period:** 1765

## Sections

### 1. The Stamp Act
My name is William, and I am sixteen years old. I'm an apprentice at a printing shop in Boston, learning the trade from Master Green. Today, March 22, 1765, news arrived that has everyone in our shop furious: Parliament has passed the Stamp Act.

### 2. Sons of Liberty
A group calling themselves the Sons of Liberty has formed to resist the Stamp Act. They meet at night, make plans, and intimidate anyone who supports the tax. Their leader is Samuel Adams, a firebrand who writes passionate articles against British tyranny.

### 3. Colonial Unity
In October, representatives from nine colonies met in New York for a Stamp Act Congress. This is remarkable—the colonies rarely cooperate. But this threat has brought us together.

### 4. The Townshend Acts
Our celebration didn't last long. In 1767, Parliament passed new taxes called the Townshend Acts, named for Charles Townshend, the British Chancellor of the Exchequer. These taxes are different from the Stamp Act—they're duties on imported goods like tea, glass, lead, paint, and paper.

## Key Vocabulary

- **Stamp Act:** A 1765 British law requiring colonists to pay a tax on printed materials by purchasing special stamped paper.
- **No Taxation Without Representation:** The colonial principle that they could only be taxed by legislatures where they had elected representatives, not by Parliament.
- **Sons of Liberty:** A colonial organization that used protests, intimidation, and sometimes violence to resist British policies like the Stamp Act.
- **Samuel Adams:** A Boston leader and organizer of colonial resistance against British taxation and policies.
- **Stamp Act Congress:** A 1765 meeting of representatives from nine colonies to coordinate opposition to the Stamp Act and assert colonial rights.
- **Boycott:** Refusing to buy certain goods as a form of protest. Colonists boycotted British goods to resist unfair taxes.
