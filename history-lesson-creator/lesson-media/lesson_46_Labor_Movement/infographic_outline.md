# Infographic Outline: Labor Movement

**Subtitle:** Workers Unite (1877-1914)

**Time Period:** 1877-1914

## Sections

### 1. The Eight-Hour Day Movement
I'm Michael, seventeen years old, and I work in a factory in Chicago making agricultural equipment. I work fourteen hours a day, six days a week. I start at 6 a.m.

### 2. The Haymarket Tragedy
May 3, 1886. At the McCormick Reaper Works, police attack striking workers, killing two. Labor activists call for a protest rally the next evening at Haymarket Square.

### 3. The AFL and Craft Unionism
After Haymarket, labor organizers change tactics. Samuel Gompers, a cigar maker, builds the American Federation of Labor (AFL) founded in 1886. The AFL takes a different approach from the Knights of Labor.

### 4. The Triangle Shirtwaist Fire
March 25, 1911. A fire breaks out at the Triangle Shirtwaist Factory in New York City. The factory occupies the top three floors of a ten-story building.

### 5. Labor's Long Struggle
By 1914, I'm forty-five years old. I've been a factory worker my entire adult life. The labor movement has made progress, but it's been slow and costly.

## Key Vocabulary

- **Haymarket Affair:** 1886 Chicago labor rally ending in bombing and violent repression of labor movement.
- **American Federation of Labor:** Moderate union organizing skilled workers, led by Samuel Gompers.
- **Samuel Gompers:** AFL leader who pursued moderate labor tactics.
- **Strike:** Workers refusing to work to pressure employers for better conditions.
- **Eight-Hour Day:** Labor movement goal to limit workday to eight hours.
- **Triangle Shirtwaist Fire:** 1911 fire killing 146 workers, spurring labor reforms.
