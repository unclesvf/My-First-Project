## Chapter 4: The Whiskey Rebellion
*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
In 1794, farmers in western Pennsylvania rebelled against Hamilton's whiskey tax. These frontier farmers grew grain and distilled it into whiskey—easier to transport than bulky grain sacks. The tax hit them hard, and they saw it as the same kind of unfair taxation Britain had imposed. The rebels attacked tax collectors, burned buildings, and threatened armed resistance.

## Chapter 5: Washington's Legacy
*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
When Washington left office in 1797, he'd served eight years as our first President. He could have served for life—many wanted him to. But he chose to step down, proving that American presidents would serve limited terms and transfer power peacefully. That decision may be his greatest gift to our republic.
