## Sections

### 1. The Kansas-Nebraska Act
I'm John, seventeen years old, and my family moved to Kansas Territory in 1855. We came from Ohio seeking good farmland and a fresh start. Kansas seemed like the perfect opportunity.

### 2. Border Ruffians and Fraud
The 1855 territorial election should have been straightforward: Kansas settlers vote on whether to allow slavery. Instead, it became a farce. Days before the election, thousands of armed Missourians—called "Border Ruffians"—crossed into Kansas.

### 3. Violence Erupts
May 1856. Pro-slavery forces attacked Lawrence, the free-state capital. They called us traitors and rebels.

### 4. Bleeding Kansas
The violence didn't stop. For the next three years, Kansas bled. Pro-slavery "Border Ruffians" and free-state "Jayhawkers" raided each other's settlements.

## Key Vocabulary

- **Kansas-Nebraska Act:** 1854 law allowing Kansas/Nebraska to decide slavery by popular sovereignty.
- **Bleeding Kansas:** Violent conflict in Kansas Territory over whether it would allow slavery.
- **Stephen Douglas:** Senator who proposed Kansas-Nebraska Act.
- **Border Ruffians:** Pro-slavery Missourians who illegally voted in Kansas.
- **John Brown:** Radical abolitionist who led violence in Kansas.
- **Pottawatomie Massacre:** John Brown's killing of five pro-slavery settlers.
