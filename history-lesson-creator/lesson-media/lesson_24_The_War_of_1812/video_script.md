Britain aids Native tribes fighting American expansion. Americans want to seize Canada.

## Chapter 3: The Burning of Washington
*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
August 1814 brought America's darkest moment in the war. A British force marched on Washington D.C., our nation's capital. Our militia tried to stop them at Bladensburg, Maryland, but we broke and ran—soldiers call it the "Bladensburg Races."

British troops entered Washington unopposed. They burned the Capitol building, where Congress meets.

## Chapter 4: The Hartford Convention
*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
Three months after we defended Baltimore, in December 1814, New England Federalists met in Hartford, Connecticut. They opposed the war from the beginning—it hurt their shipping trade with Britain. Some talked openly of New England leaving the Union. The Hartford Convention proposed constitutional amendments to limit federal power: require two-thirds vote to declare war, admit new states, or restrict trade.

*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
The war's strangest moment came in January 1815. Andrew Jackson, commanding American forces in New Orleans, faced a battle British army. On January 8, British troops in neat formations charged American positions behind cotton bales and earthworks. It was a slaughter.

*Duration: 1-2 minutes*

**Narration:**
