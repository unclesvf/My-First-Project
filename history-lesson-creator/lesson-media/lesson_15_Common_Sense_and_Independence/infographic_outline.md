### 3. The Vote for Independence
On July 2, 1776, the Continental Congress voted for independence. Twelve colonies voted yes; New York abstained but later agreed. July 2 is actually Independence Day, though we'll celebrate July 4, when the Declaration was formally adopted.

### 4. Revolutionary Ideas
The Declaration is more than a list of complaints against King George. It's a statement of revolutionary political philosophy drawn from Enlightenment thinkers like John Locke. The ideas it expresses will echo through history.

### 5. The Price of Freedom
After the signing, Benjamin Franklin reportedly said, "We must all hang together, or most assuredly we shall all hang separately." He meant it literally. By signing the Declaration, fifty-six men committed treason against Britain. If we lose this war, they'll be executed.

## Key Vocabulary

- **Common Sense:** A 1776 pamphlet by Thomas Paine arguing for American independence in plain language that influenced public opinion.
- **Declaration of Independence:** The 1776 document declaring American independence and explaining the philosophical basis for revolution.
- **Thomas Jefferson:** Primary author of the Declaration of Independence, expressing Enlightenment ideals about rights and government.
- **Unalienable Rights:** Rights that cannot be taken away, including life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness according to the Declaration.
- **All Men Are Created Equal:** A principle from the Declaration stating that all people have equal value and rights, though not practiced in reality.
- **Thomas Paine:** Author of Common Sense, which persuaded many colonists to support independence from Britain.
